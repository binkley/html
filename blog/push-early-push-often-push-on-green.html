Prologue

I'm chatting with a colleague who is new in her role as an Agile Coach; she comes from a PM background. We were talking about ways to organize a team's story board (card wall), and turned to Desk Checks, and when they happen in a story's lifecycle.

An interesting remark by her: an earlier team she had been on agreed to share work (push) only if the desk check were successful! That is, they do not push code until a story is almost done, and lots of work lies fallow on individuals' machines in the meanwhile.

I was floored. The team she had been on was a strong agile team, with experienced members. Why would they wait to push until days of work had passed? What did they do about merge conflicts, complex refactorings, integration failures in the pipeline, et al?
