Prologue

I'm chatting with a colleague who is new in her role as an Agile Coach; she comes from a PM background. We were talking about ways to organize a team's story board (card wall), and turned to Desk Checks and when they fit into a story's life.

An interesting remark by her: a team she had once been on had agreed to share work (push) only after the desk check was successful. That is, they did not push code until a story was almost done: work lay fallow on individuals' machines pending desk check.

I was floored. The team she had been on was a strong agile team with experienced members. Why would they wait to push until days of work had passed? What did they do about merge conflicts, complex refactorings, integration failures in the pipeline, et al?

Source of my dismay

In my way of thinking, this was clearly a smell. Martin Fowler specifically addresses this.
